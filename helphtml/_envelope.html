envelope: A control signal generator that generates a signal that is usually triggered at the start of a note, rises to a peak value that may vary with velocity or other things, then optionally decay toward zero, and finally decay toward zero when the note is released. Each voice has two basic envelopes, plus two more that can also be delayed or repetitively triggered by sweep generators or other things. These latter two can also be computed by the virtual instrument, for envelopes that are common to all voices, for effects like simulating Hammond organ percussion.

Also: glide, sweep
