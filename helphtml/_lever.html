lever: One of the two controllers to the left of the keyboard on the Digital Chroma. The control signals generated by them can be used to modulate anything, but typically the left lever is used to control sweep amplitude, and indirectly vibrato, while the right is used to modulate oscillator pitch, for pitch bend. This assignment works well because the thumb and forefinger can bend the pitch either way, while the middle finger can pull for vibrato.

Note that each side of a lever’s range can be used as a separate modulation source, with names starting with “–lever” or “+lever”.

The physical polarities of the levers (which direction is considered positive) can be set by global parameters according to the player’s preference.
