amplifier: A sound processing module within a voice that controls the volume. Each voice has two, one in each channel. Each amplifier’s volume is controlled by the sum of two envelope mod inputs, multiplied by a post mod input typically used for a pedal or a sweep tremolo.

An amplifier is usually used at the output of the voice, in which case it also includes a pan control that can be modulated. But the B channel amplifier is used in certain patches to control the mix of the A and B oscillators, in which case the pan portion is disabled.

Also: oscillator, waveshaper, filter
