unit: A quantity that represents the scaling of a control signal value. For note pitch and glide output, one unit equals 32 semitones, so the Digital Chroma’s keyboard is two units long. Sweep signals always have a +1 unit maximum values, but some shapes are unipolar while others are bipolar. Envelopes go from zero to one if fixed amplitude, or up to two if modulated by velocity or pedal. Levers are ±1 unit, or 0 to 1 unit if only one side of the lever is used. Pedals are 0 to 1 unit. Finally, modulation depths are documented in terms of how much they vary what they modulate for each unit of input.
